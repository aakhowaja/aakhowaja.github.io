FINAL
As we progressed on our design ideas, there was a lack of visual cohesiveness between elements. To address this issue, we created a resource web and an early SketchUp model depicting how various engineering design elements came together to support the park as a whole.
MID STAGE
During the early stages, we each developed our own ideas and came together to create an organically formed layout that connected the city grid to the fluidity of the Hudson River. One goal of the park was to help showcase the food and art of local chefs and sculptors.
PRELIMINARY
Our inspiration was derived from a variety of old and new public spaces. These precedents included Klyde Warren Park located in Dallas, United States, Piazza San Marco located in Venice, Italy, and Garden City Shopping Mall located in Sau Paulo, Brazil.
INSPIRATION
Gansevoort Peninsula was offered to be redeveloped into an iconic park. Located in the meatpacking district and home to the NY Dept. of Sanitation and Fire Dept., the site was not well maintained as evident by the large amounts of trash that littered its coast.
