BUS STATION COMPLEX


Fallujah
Anbar Province, Iraq
10 | 2017

Background

The city of Fallujah, Iraq has undergone significant cultural, economic, and political changes as ISIS exerted its influence in the town over the several year. Remnants of war and destruction litter the city streets, reminding local residents of the violence which brought the town to a standstill. With this in mind, Fallujah Bus Station is purpose-built to inspire hope and serve as regional icon, symbolic of the post-war reconstruction. Locally sourced designs and materials aim to create jobs and emphasize a community-led approach to the development of this structure located in near Fallujah's central market.

Preliminary

The design of the bus station and its adjacent complex were primarily influenced by Islamic architecture ubiquitously found across the country and region. Islamic geometric patterns including hexagons and octagons were combined with a unique palette of vibrant colors to create the building's vernacular design. The hexagon and octagon inspired design (shown in the middle image) was also the most viable option due to its structural efficiency and proportion to the site.

Mid-Stage

Building layouts were finalized during this step and design elements were integrated into the framework of the bus station. Hand-drawings were translated to virtual design softwares including SketchUp and AutoCAD to better communicate the details and scale of this project.

Final

The Bus Station Complex aims to create a culturally vibrant space that facilities social interaction between people, and catalyzes intercity movement by offering affordable transportation for refugees moving back to the newly liberated city.
