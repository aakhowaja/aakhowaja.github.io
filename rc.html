Our planned ideas cohesively joined together to create a safe refugee camp (adjacent to the Euphrates River) that efficiently used space, minimized environmental impact, and embraced aspects of local community and culture.
FINAL
To combat intense heat, covered water tanks were placed in high-demand areas to store water transported from the central filtration station.
Water turbines and power lines were also set up to generate and distribute electricity.
PRELIMINARY
Refugee camps set up in the region provided a significantly lower quality of life than a home environment. Understanding the spatial distribution of Fallujah, Iraq led us to create our own refugee camp aimed to cultivate a greater sense of community.
